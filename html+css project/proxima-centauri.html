Proxima Centauri – Our Closest Stellar Neighbor

Proxima Centauri is a faint red dwarf star located just 4.24 light-years from Earth. Despite its low brightness, it's one of the most intriguing stars due to its proximity and exoplanet system.

Quick Facts About Proxima Centauri

  • Type: Red dwarf (M-type)
  • Distance from Earth: 4.24 light-years
  • Brightness: Magnitude 11 (not visible to the naked eye)
  • Constellation: Centaurus
  • Part of: Alpha Centauri star system

Small but Important

Proxima Centauri is a **low-mass, cool, and dim red dwarf**, making it invisible without a telescope. Although tiny compared to our Sun, it's the closest known star to Earth — making it an important object of study.

Exoplanets Around Proxima

  • Proxima b: An Earth-sized planet located in the habitable zone, where liquid water might exist.
  • Proxima c: A larger super-Earth or mini-Neptune, orbiting further out.

The discovery of these planets makes Proxima Centauri a **top target for future interstellar missions** and alien life studies.

Solar Activity and Flares

Proxima is known for its intense **stellar flares**, which release powerful radiation and may impact the atmospheres of its planets. These flares make habitability more challenging but not impossible.

Part of the Alpha Centauri System

Though Proxima Centauri is the closest star to us, it's gravitationally bound to **Alpha Centauri A** and **Alpha Centauri B**, forming a **triple star system**.

Fun Facts

  • Discovered in 1915 by Robert Innes.
  • It is only about 12% the mass of the Sun.
  • Despite being closest, it’s 500 times less luminous than the Sun.
  • Mission concepts like **Breakthrough Starshot** aim to send tiny spacecraft to Proxima Centauri.
