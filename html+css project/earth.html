Earth – Our Living Planet

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known world to support life. With oceans, a breathable atmosphere, and a magnetic field, Earth is a unique oasis in the cosmos.

Introduction to Earth

Earth is the only planet known to harbor life. It's about 4.5 billion years old and orbits the Sun at an average distance of about 150 million kilometers (93 million miles). Its balanced climate, magnetic field, protective atmosphere, and presence of water make it unlike any other planet we've discovered.

Planetary Structure

Earth is made up of several layers:

  • Crust: The solid outer layer, ranging from 5 to 70 km thick.
  • Mantle: A hot, semi-solid layer beneath the crust responsible for plate tectonics.
  • Outer Core: Liquid iron and nickel that generates Earth’s magnetic field.
  • Inner Core: A solid sphere of iron and nickel, reaching temperatures as hot as the Sun's surface.

Earth's Atmosphere

The atmosphere is a mixture of nitrogen (78%), oxygen (21%), and trace gases including carbon dioxide and argon. It protects life by blocking harmful radiation and moderating temperature extremes.

  • Troposphere: Where all weather occurs (up to ~12 km).
  • Stratosphere: Contains the ozone layer (~12–50 km).
  • Mesosphere, Thermosphere, and Exosphere: Extend far into space, helping absorb solar radiation and prevent meteors from reaching the surface.

Surface Features

Earth's surface is 71% water and 29% land, featuring oceans, continents, mountains, rivers, deserts, and polar ice caps. It's the only planet with liquid water on the surface.

  • Continents: Seven large land masses: Asia, Africa, North America, South America, Antarctica, Europe, and Australia.
  • Oceans: Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Southern, and Arctic.
  • Highest Point: Mount Everest (8,848 m).
  • Deepest Point: Mariana Trench (~11,000 m).

Earth’s Unique Traits

  • Liquid Water: Essential for life, Earth’s oceans help regulate climate and temperature.
  • Plate Tectonics: Drives the movement of continents, formation of mountains, and earthquakes.
  • Magnetic Field: Protects Earth from solar and cosmic radiation.
  • Seasons: Caused by Earth’s 23.5° axial tilt during its orbit around the Sun.
  • One Moon: Stabilizes Earth’s rotation and affects tides.

Earth and Life

Earth supports over 8.7 million known species — from microscopic bacteria to giant whales. Its biosphere includes every living organism and the ecosystems they inhabit: forests, oceans, grasslands, and polar regions.

Exploration and Observation

  • Satellites: Used for weather, communication, environmental monitoring, and GPS.
  • Space Stations: Like the International Space Station (ISS), orbit Earth and support long-term research in microgravity.
  • Earth Observation: NASA, ESA, and other agencies monitor Earth's health, climate change, and natural disasters.

Fun Facts About Earth

  1. Earth is the only planet not named after a god.
  2. The Moon is slowly drifting away from Earth (~3.8 cm per year).
  3. Earth’s rotation is gradually slowing down — a day gets longer by ~1.7 milliseconds per century.
  4. Over 90% of Earth's living biomass is in plants.
  5. Earth experiences about 100 lightning strikes per second globally.
  6. Earth’s atmosphere allows visible light but blocks most harmful ultraviolet rays.
