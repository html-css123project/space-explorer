Messier 87 – The Giant Elliptical Galaxy

Messier 87 (M87) is a massive elliptical galaxy best known for its gigantic black hole, the first ever imaged by humanity. It lies at the heart of the Virgo Cluster.

What Is Messier 87?

Messier 87 is a supergiant elliptical galaxy located around 53.5 million light-years away in the constellation Virgo. It is one of the most massive galaxies known, with a powerful black hole at its core that emits jets of high-energy particles.

Basic Facts

  • Type: Elliptical galaxy (E0–E1)
  • Distance from Earth: ~53.5 million light-years
  • Location: Constellation Virgo
  • Diameter: ~120,000 light-years
  • Stars: Several hundred billion
  • Black Hole Mass: ~6.5 billion solar masses

Supermassive Black Hole

The central black hole of M87 is among the largest known. In April 2019, the Event Horizon Telescope captured the first-ever image of a black hole — M87’s — showing the glowing ring of hot plasma surrounding it.

The black hole produces a massive jet of energetic particles that extends over 5,000 light-years into space.

Structure and Composition

Unlike spiral galaxies, M87 is elliptical with no distinct arms. Its shape is smooth and filled with older stars. The galaxy contains:

  • Globular Clusters: Over 12,000 — far more than the Milky Way.
  • Hot Gas Halo: Emits strong X-rays visible to space telescopes.
  • Dark Matter: Essential for gravitational stability.

Part of the Virgo Cluster

M87 is a dominant member of the Virgo Cluster, a collection of over 1,300 galaxies. It plays a key gravitational role in shaping the cluster’s structure and interactions.

Fun Facts About Messier 87

  1. M87's black hole was the first ever photographed — a breakthrough in astrophysics.
  2. Its jet is powered by material falling into the black hole and moving near light speed.
  3. It has more globular clusters than almost any other known galaxy.
  4. M87 is so massive it warps nearby galaxies through gravity.
  5. It was discovered by Charles Messier in 1781.
