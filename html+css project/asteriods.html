The Rocky Wanderers: Asteroids

Asteroids are rocky leftovers from the formation of our solar system. These space rocks orbit the Sun, mostly in the Asteroid Belt between Mars and Jupiter. They hold clues to how planets were formed.

What Are Asteroids?

Asteroids are small, rocky objects that orbit the Sun but are too small to be called planets. They range from a few meters wide to hundreds of kilometers across. Most reside in the Main Asteroid Belt, but others exist near Earth or in other parts of the solar system.

Types of Asteroids

  • C-type (Carbonaceous): Dark, rich in carbon, and the most common.
  • S-type (Silicaceous): Made of silicate and nickel-iron.
  • M-type (Metallic): Composed mostly of metals like nickel and iron.

Famous Asteroids

Asteroid Key Info
Ceres Largest asteroid; also classified as a dwarf planet.
Vesta One of the brightest asteroids; has a huge crater at its south pole.
Apophis Near-Earth asteroid that once raised concerns about a future impact.
Bennu Target of NASA’s OSIRIS-REx mission to collect asteroid samples.

Cool Asteroid Facts

  1. There are over 1 million known asteroids in our solar system.
  2. Some asteroids have their own moons.
  3. Asteroids are ancient — more than 4.5 billion years old.
  4. Metal-rich asteroids could one day be mined for resources.
  5. Asteroid impacts have shaped Earth’s surface — including the one that wiped out dinosaurs.
  6. NASA tracks near-Earth objects (NEOs) to monitor potential threats.

Asteroids vs Comets

Feature Asteroids Comets
Location Mostly in the Asteroid Belt Kuiper Belt & Oort Cloud
Composition Rock and metal Ice, dust, rock
Tail No Yes, near the Sun
Surface Solid, rocky Icy and volatile
