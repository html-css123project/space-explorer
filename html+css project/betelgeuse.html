Betelgeuse – A Supergiant Near Its End

Betelgeuse is a red supergiant located in the constellation Orion. It’s nearing the end of its life and may soon explode in a spectacular supernova.

Quick Facts About Betelgeuse

  • Star Type: Red supergiant
  • Constellation: Orion
  • Distance from Earth: ~642.5 light-years
  • Diameter: ~1,400 times the Sun
  • Color: Deep orange-red

A Giant Nearing Death

Betelgeuse is in the final stage of its stellar life. It has exhausted most of its hydrogen fuel and now fuses heavier elements in its core. Eventually, this will lead to a **supernova explosion**, and Betelgeuse will collapse into a neutron star or black hole.

How Big Is Betelgeuse?

Betelgeuse is **so large** that if placed in our Solar System, it would extend beyond the orbit of **Jupiter**! Despite its size, its density is low — you could fly a spaceship through its outer layers.

Brightness and Variability

Betelgeuse is a **variable star**, meaning its brightness changes over time. In late 2019 and early 2020, it mysteriously dimmed, leading many to speculate it might go supernova — but this was later attributed to a dust cloud and surface cooling.

When Will It Explode?

Scientists estimate Betelgeuse may go supernova **any time within the next 100,000 years** — that’s “soon” in cosmic terms! When it does, it will shine **brighter than the full Moon** for weeks, but the explosion will not be harmful to Earth.

Cultural Significance

  • Known since ancient times and referenced in Egyptian, Greek, and Aboriginal star lore.
  • One of the “shoulders” of Orion in the famous constellation.
  • Its distinct reddish glow makes it easy to identify in the night sky.

Fun Facts About Betelgeuse

  • It's one of the few stars large enough to have its surface imaged by telescopes.
  • Betelgeuse loses mass rapidly, creating a vast cloud of gas around it.
  • Its name comes from Arabic and means "hand of Orion."
