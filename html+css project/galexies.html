Galaxies – Islands in the Universe

Galaxies are vast systems of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter held together by gravity. Each one is a universe of its own, filled with billions — even trillions — of stars.

What Is a Galaxy?

A galaxy is a gigantic system of stars, star systems, star clusters, gas, dust, and dark matter, all bound together by gravity. The universe contains more than 100 billion galaxies, each with its own unique shape, size, and history.

Types of Galaxies

  • Spiral Galaxies: Have a central bulge with arms winding outward — like our Milky Way.
  • Elliptical Galaxies: Oval-shaped and mostly made up of older stars; little gas or dust.
  • Irregular Galaxies: No defined shape; often formed by collisions or gravitational interactions.
  • Lenticular Galaxies: A mix between spiral and elliptical galaxies.

Famous Galaxies

Galaxy Key Facts
Milky Way Our home galaxy; contains over 100 billion stars.
Andromeda Nearest major galaxy to the Milky Way; will collide with it in ~4.5 billion years.
Messier 87 Huge elliptical galaxy; home to the first photographed black hole.
Large Magellanic Cloud Satellite galaxy of the Milky Way; visible from the Southern Hemisphere.
Sombrero Galaxy Has a bright central bulge and a dark dust lane — shaped like a sombrero hat.

Mind-Blowing Galaxy Facts

  1. The Milky Way is about 100,000 light-years wide.
  2. Galaxies collide and merge — it’s a common part of cosmic evolution.
  3. Elliptical galaxies are often found in galaxy clusters.
  4. There are dwarf galaxies that contain just a few billion stars.
  5. Dark matter makes up most of the mass in galaxies, even though we can't see it.
  6. Galaxies can host supermassive black holes at their centers.
  7. Some galaxies are so far away, we see them as they were billions of years ago!

Galaxies vs Solar Systems

Feature Galaxy Solar System
Size Hundreds of thousands of light-years A few light-hours across
Contents Billions of stars, planets, gas, dust, dark matter One star (the Sun), planets, moons, asteroids, comets
Location Exists in the universe Exists within a galaxy
Examples Milky Way, Andromeda Our Solar System
