The Universe – All That Exists

The universe contains everything: space, time, matter, energy, stars, planets, galaxies, and beyond.

What Is the Universe?

The universe is the totality of everything that exists — from the tiniest subatomic particles to massive clusters of galaxies. It includes all of space and time, and it is expanding outward from a single origin known as the **Big Bang**.

Origin – The Big Bang

Scientists believe the universe began around **13.8 billion years ago** in an extremely hot, dense state. This event, called the **Big Bang**, started the ongoing expansion of space itself.

What Is in the Universe?

  • Galaxies: Vast systems of stars, planets, dust, and gas. Learn more about a galaxy.
  • Stars & Planets: From our own Sun and Earth to billions of others.
  • Dark Matter & Dark Energy: Invisible substances that make up most of the universe.
  • Nebulae: Giant clouds of gas where stars are born.
  • Black Holes: Extremely dense regions with gravity so strong, not even light escapes.

The Expanding Universe

The universe is not static — it’s growing. Galaxies are moving away from each other as space stretches. This discovery by **Edwin Hubble** supported the Big Bang theory and changed how we view the cosmos.

Observable vs. Entire Universe

The **observable universe** is the part we can see, about **93 billion light-years across**. The full universe could be far larger, possibly even infinite.

Fun Facts

  • The universe has no known center or edge.
  • There are more galaxies in the universe than grains of sand on Earth’s beaches.
  • Time and space are linked in what’s called space-time.
  • Scientists estimate the universe is made of: 5% normal matter, 27% dark matter, and 68% dark energy.
