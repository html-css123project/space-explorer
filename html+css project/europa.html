Europa – The Icy Moon of Jupiter

Europa, one of Jupiter’s largest moons, is a world of ice and mystery. Beneath its frozen crust lies a deep, salty ocean that may contain the ingredients for life.

Basic Facts About Europa

  • Parent Planet: Jupiter
  • Diameter: 3,121.6 km (slightly smaller than Earth’s Moon)
  • Surface: Smooth icy shell with cracks and ridges
  • Atmosphere: Thin, mostly oxygen (not breathable)
  • Orbital Period: About 3.5 Earth days

What Makes Europa Special?

Europa is one of the most promising places in the Solar System to search for alien life. Scientists believe an ocean of liquid water lies beneath its icy shell, kept warm by tidal heating caused by Jupiter's gravity.

The icy surface has very few impact craters, indicating it is relatively young and geologically active. Huge cracks and ridges suggest subsurface movement of water or slushy ice.

Possibility of Life

If Europa’s ocean contains hydrothermal vents on the seafloor — like those on Earth — it could support microbial life. Its salty waters, energy sources, and chemical ingredients create a potentially habitable environment.

Europa in Mythology

The moon is named after Europa, a Phoenician noblewoman in Greek mythology who was abducted by Zeus (Jupiter) in the form of a bull. Many of Jupiter’s moons are named after his mythological lovers or children.

Past and Future Missions

  • Galileo (1995–2003): Provided detailed data on Europa’s surface and magnetic field.
  • Juno (ongoing): Provides glimpses of Europa while orbiting Jupiter.
  • Europa Clipper (planned for launch in 2024): Will orbit Jupiter and make multiple close flybys of Europa to investigate its ocean and habitability.

Cool Facts About Europa

  • Europa's ice shell is estimated to be 15–25 km thick.
  • Its subsurface ocean might be 60–150 km deep — deeper than Earth’s oceans.
  • Europa reflects about 64% of sunlight — it's one of the brightest moons.
  • The cracked surface resembles sea ice on Earth’s polar oceans.
  • It may have icy geysers that spray water into space.
