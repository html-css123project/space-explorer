Apophis – The Close-Passing Asteroid

99942 Apophis is a near-Earth asteroid that once caused global concern — but is now closely studied for science and safety.

Quick Facts About Apophis

  • Type: Near-Earth Asteroid (Aten group)
  • Discovered: June 19, 2004, by Roy Tucker, David Tholen, and Fabrizio Bernardi
  • Size: Approximately 340 meters in diameter
  • Closest Approach: April 13, 2029 (less than 32,000 km from Earth)
  • Orbital Period: ~323 days

Why Was Apophis Feared?

After its discovery, early calculations showed a small chance that Apophis might strike Earth in 2029 or 2036. The asteroid was briefly placed at level 4 on the Torino Impact Scale — the highest ever assigned to any object.

Thankfully, further observations ruled out any threat for the next 100 years.

Close Encounter in 2029

On April 13, 2029, Apophis will fly past Earth at an incredibly close distance — closer than many satellites! It will be visible to the naked eye in parts of Europe, Africa, and Asia.

This historic event provides scientists a unique opportunity to study its shape, spin, and orbit using radar and telescopes.

NASA’s Plans for Apophis

NASA is planning observations during and after the 2029 flyby to understand how close approaches affect asteroid paths, structure, and rotation. A future mission may even visit Apophis to observe it directly.

Did You Know?

  • Apophis is named after the Egyptian god of chaos.
  • It orbits the Sun faster than Earth — every 0.88 years.
  • It was once the highest-risk asteroid ever recorded.
  • Its 2029 flyby will help refine impact modeling for planetary defense.
  • It is not expected to impact Earth for at least the next century.
