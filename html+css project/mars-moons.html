Phobos & Deimos – The Tiny Moons of Mars

Mars has two small, potato-shaped moons: Phobos and Deimos. These mysterious bodies are likely captured asteroids and have intrigued scientists for decades.

Overview: Small and Strange

Unlike Earth's round and large Moon, **Phobos** and **Deimos** are tiny and irregularly shaped. They may be captured asteroids from the **asteroid belt**, pulled in by Mars’ gravity long ago.

Phobos – The Doomed Moon

  • Size: ~22 km wide
  • Orbital Distance: ~6,000 km (closer than any other moon in the solar system)
  • Orbit Time: 7.6 hours (orbits Mars faster than Mars rotates!)
  • Surface: Covered in dust and deep craters; most famous is the Stickney Crater
  • Future: Spiraling inward and expected to crash into Mars or break apart in 30–50 million years

Because Phobos orbits Mars so quickly, it rises in the west and sets in the east — the opposite of what we see with our Moon.

Deimos – The Distant Companion

  • Size: ~12 km wide
  • Orbital Distance: ~23,000 km from Mars
  • Orbit Time: ~30.3 hours
  • Surface: Smoother than Phobos with fewer large craters
  • Appearance: Looks like a lumpy rock covered in regolith (fine dust)

Deimos orbits farther away and moves slowly across the sky. It's harder to observe and resembles a distant star.

Mythological Names

In Greek mythology, **Phobos** means "fear" and **Deimos** means "terror" — both were sons of the god Ares (Mars in Roman mythology). They accompanied him into battle, just as these moons accompany Mars in space.

Exploration and Interest

  • First Observed: 1877 by Asaph Hall using the U.S. Naval Observatory
  • Visited by: Viking orbiters, Mars Express, and MAVEN
  • Future Missions: Japan’s JAXA mission “Martian Moons eXploration (MMX)” aims to land on Phobos, collect samples, and return them to Earth

Fun Facts

  • Phobos is so close to Mars that it may be torn apart by gravity in the future.
  • Deimos is one of the smallest moons in the Solar System.
  • Phobos completes three orbits around Mars each Martian day!
  • Both moons may contain valuable clues about early Solar System objects.
